### BEP Research Context
- **The Other Memory Wall, Part 2** (March 4, 2026) — Apple's three-layer AI architecture (on-device, Private Cloud Compute, partner/Gemini). Four converging data points: M5 Pro/Max chiplet, PCC upgraded to M5, Qwen3.5-397B on M3 Ultra at 38 tok/s, Qwen 3.5 2B on iPhone 17 Pro. Same silicon DNA from phone to server rack = infrastructure moat.
- Key insight: Apple skipped M3 and M4 for PCC — went straight to M5. Signals PCC is core strategic infrastructure, not a side project.
- Key insight: M5 Ultra (256GB unified memory, ~1.2TB/s fused bandwidth) will be the "desktop supercomputer for inference" — enables 400B+ at 4-bit or 120B+ at full FP16 in a single machine.
- Key insight: Three-layer architecture (on-device / PCC / Gemini) is a division of labor, not a concession. Apple captures the vast majority of inference on hardware it controls completely. Gemini is overflow, not dependency.
- Investment angle: NVIDIA owns one side of the memory wall; Apple is building a moat on the other. Privacy enforced by hardware architecture (not just policy) creates structural enterprise advantage.
- [BEP Research](https://bepresearch.substack.com/p/the-other-memory-wall-part-2)
